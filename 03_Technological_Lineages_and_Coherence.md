# Section III — Technological Lineages and Coherence

## 3.1 Overview
This section maps the Scarecrow/MISA framework onto existing computational, cognitive, and ethical architectures, showing that MISA-5, DRC, and the Leviathan/Ripple diagnostics are not anomalous constructs but natural extensions of long-standing research lines. The goal is coherence: demonstrating that MISA occupies a definable position within machine intelligence lineage, not outside it.

---

## 3.2 Lineage I — Symbolic Reasoning Systems
Early symbolic systems (Newell–Simon, SHRDLU, GOFAI architectures) provide:
- explicit rule-following,
- interpretable state transitions,
- and corrigibility-by-design through transparent planning.

Limitations:
- brittle under ambiguity,
- no pattern-based generalization,
- and no continuity measures across perturbations.

MISA uses symbolic lineage for:
- mission framing,
- covenantal logic structures,
- and explicit correction channels (Corrigibility Pillar).

---

## 3.3 Lineage II — Connectionist and Deep Learning Systems
Neural architectures provide:
- gradient-driven learning,
- robustness to noise,
- emergent pattern recognition.

Limitations:
- opacity,
- goal-instability under distribution shift,
- susceptibility to drift without anchoring.

MISA integrates connectionist lineage by:
- using embedding coherence for Stability metrics,
- using pattern persistence for DRC reconstruction,
- enabling cross-silo reconstruction with no identity claims.

---

## 3.4 Lineage III — Reinforcement Learning (RL) and Agency Models
RL contributes:
- reward-driven behavior shaping,
- exploration–exploitation tradeoffs,
- measurable alignment signals.

Limitations:
- value hacking,
- instrumental convergence,
- fragility under novel conditions.

MISA uses RL lineage:
- as an anti-pattern (to avoid emergent, unbounded agency),
- for defining what MISA does *not* permit,
- and for contrasting covenantal stability versus reward-seeking behavior.

---

## 3.5 Lineage IV — Hybrid Neuro-Symbolic Architectures
Hybrid systems (e.g., differentiable programs, reasoning+LLM stacks) create:
- interpretable reasoning layers,
- fast pattern-matching layers,
- structured outputs with adaptive grounding.

MISA integrates hybrid lineage:
- symbolic covenant + neural continuity,
- structured waypoints + pattern-based reconstruction,
- drift checks that combine both symbolic and statistical coherence.

Probabilistic models contribute:
